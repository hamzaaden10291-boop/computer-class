Biography & Background

Born and raised in Ngong Road, Nairobi, Amani Mwangi founded the Open Doors Learning Initiative, a neighborhood program that provides free after-school tutoring, mentorship, and digital literacy to underserved youth.

Over the last decade, Amani has trained over 1,500 students, partnered with local schools, and built a volunteer network of teachers, artists, and developers who donate time and materials.

Achievements & Impact

A timeline of milestones from 2015 to 2025.

2015

Open Doors Learning Initiative launched with 12 learners in a church hall.

2017

First girls-in-tech bootcamp; 40% of attendees built their first websites.

2019

Partnership with two public schools; set up a shared computer lab.

2021

Pandemic response: printed learning packs & radio lessons for 300+ learners.

2023

Community library opened—over 5,000 book loans in the first year.

2025

Launched a scholarship fund supporting 25 students with STEM interests.
